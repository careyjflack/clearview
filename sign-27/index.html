Sign № 027 · Lot — Abe Lincoln Trading Company

Lot — Abe Lincoln Trading Company

The corner where Clearview announced itself to the world — named for Lincoln, founded by Mvskoke Freedmen, and humming with commerce from the town's very first days.

Businesses on this Sign

Abe Lincoln Trading Company

General store, real estate, produce

After J. A. Roper, Lemuel Jackson, and John Grayson platted the town of Clearview, they formed the Lincoln Townsite Company to advertise the community throughout the South.

Roper and Grayson also created the Abe Lincoln Trading Company to operate a general store, deal in farm produce, and real estate. Roper also owned a sawmill and lumberyard. In the early 1910s Roper moved back to Okmulgee. A formerly enslaved person, Roper had previously been a postmaster in Arkansas and Okmulgee, a delegate to the Sequoyah Convention, and among a group of African American leaders to travel to Washington to persuade President Theodore Roosevelt to not approve Oklahoma Statehood.

In 1914 J. E. Thompson moved to Clearview and took over many of the town promotions and development that Roper and Grayson had done. He purchased town lots and land in the area and financed the town newspaper, the Clearview Patriarch.

Restaurant / Pool Hall

Restaurant and pool hall

Tom Carpenter sold candies, ciders, and more at his confectionary on Main Street.

In 1913 he also advertised that he sold barbecued meats. The stand was operating at least as early as 1911 and the last confirmed date is 1914, though it likely lasted longer.

Clearview Patriot — Printing Office, Restaurant & BBQ, Gas Station

Newspaper, restaurant, filling station

Earl and Mallie French opened a filling station on this corner in 1927.

A 1929 advertisement lists the Earl French Station as one of 32 filling stations in the area offering Conoco Ethyl Gasoline as distributed by the Okemah Oil and Gasoline Co. The presence of a gas station implies the ownership of enough cars in Clearview to provide this type of service. Frank Bush ran it after them.

John Grayson and M. C. Alford are also listed as distributors of Weleetka Oil and Gas Co. Ethyl Gasoline and Empire Gasoline in a January 1930 newspaper advertisement.
