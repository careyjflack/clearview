M.J. Blanks Dry Goods & Notions

The Blanks family ran a dry goods store that sold a little of everything — a quiet, essential presence in Clearview's commercial life through the 1910s.

History

In the 1910s, N. and M. J. Blanks operated a grocery and dry goods store in Clearview, Oklahoma, at a time when the town was a busy all-Black community built around farming, local trade, and the Fort Smith and Western Railroad.

Their store likely served as more than a place to buy supplies; it would have been a daily stop for families coming in from nearby farms to purchase flour, sugar, coffee, cloth, thread, shoes, tools, and other household necessities. Like many Clearview businesses of the period, the Blanks store did a little of everything. In addition to groceries and dry goods, the business bought and sold produce and livestock, helping connect local farmers to the town's economy.

The store also operated a millinery, offering hats and related goods at a time when carefully dressed appearances were important for church, school programs, social gatherings, and business dealings. In a town that supported newspapers, churches, schools, general stores, and other enterprises, grocery stores such as the Blanks family business helped hold community life together.

A December 1911 advertisement in the Patriarch lists a range of items in stock for patrons to pick before Christmas. Items include trumpets, bugles, vases, fireworks, doll beds, pianos, cups, saucers, mugs, bureaus, fancy boxes, dolls, drums, etc. The holiday advertisements and range of items reveal a significant consumer economy in Clearview in the years leading up to the First World War.

The last known mention of the store appeared in the Clearview Patriarch in 1914, but its presence reflects the ambition and self-sufficiency that shaped Clearview during its early years.
