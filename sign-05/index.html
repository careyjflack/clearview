Jeff Horn Dry Good Store

Dry goods
Owner
Jeff Horn (1875 – 1981)
Operated
1920s
Type
Dry goods store

Born in Louisiana in 1875, Jeff Horn migrated to Clearview and eventually opened two different grocery stores.

He purchased one lot in 1920 and sold the store and its contents in 1925. A July 1921 article in the Black Dispatch noted that he offered ice cream and cold drinks. Horn died in 1981.

Earl French Pool Hall

Pool hall
Owner
Earl French (1896 – 1983)
Operated
ca. 1940s – 1950s
Type
Pool hall

Located at the corner of Main and Langston, Earl French's Pool Hall provided entertainment to residents young and old through the middle of the twentieth century.

Earl French, a long-time Clearview resident dating back to the 1910s, caught a significant break in the 1920s when oil was found on his land. He would use the money he received from the oil wells to open a filling station and store, manage real estate, farm, purchase a bus, run a taxi service, and eventually run a pool hall. These establishments were strictly regulated to discourage gambling or illegal sale of alcohol and Earl French ran into trouble running an unlicensed pool hall in Weleetka in 1937.

At the Clearview pool hall he would allow schoolchildren to socialize after school and become an adult-only establishment in the evenings. Earl French worked as a bus driver for Clearview schools from 1948–1957 and ran a taxi service bussing people across the state to attend church programs, funerals, as well as to harvest cotton in western Oklahoma. His entrepreneurial spirit provided essential services for Clearview residents and underscores the opportunities available in all-Black towns.
