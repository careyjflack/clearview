Restaurant / Pool Hall

History

There were several restaurants and pool halls along Main Street in Clearview and not much is known about this particular location.

Beer joints and pool halls were relatively clandestine places during this time as Oklahoma's strict prohibition laws did not allow the sale of beer until 1933 and hard liquor until 1959. Undeterred, various Clearview residents made or transported beer, choc beer, and moonshine whiskey as a way to make some extra income. As a result, pool halls commonly show up in newspaper articles related to raids on illicit gambling or possession of liquor, with reports as early as 1910 of raids on pool halls owned by Clearview residents.

Racial prejudice may have played a role in some of these arrests. In a 1914 article in the Okfuskee County News it was stated, "Negroes, pool table and dice are strong presumptive evidence of a crap game." In this instance, the jury disagreed and acquitted the defendants.

Pool halls were apparently so profitable that a deputy sheriff involved with raiding pool halls in Clearview would later retire and open his own pool hall in Okemah.

Third Post Office Location

History

The first U.S. Post Office was established September 30, 1902. In 1904 an order was issued by the U.S. Postal Department changing the name Clearview Post Office to Abe Lincoln Post Office, but was rescinded a month later, retaining the original name.

John Grayson was named the first postmaster. Those who succeeded him were N. H. Starks, M. W. (Wash) Irving, Mary Doggett, Mattie E. Lucas and Imogene Chilton Bruton. From the Clearview Post Office, the outlying area was served by a rural route carrier. The first rural route mail carrier was Ellis Washington, followed by O. J. Simms, L. H. Haynes, J. P. Owens, Robert (Bob) Burden, and Jimmie Burden. J. P. Owens served as rural route carrier from 1927 to 1974.

During the early 1970s the Clearview and Weleetka routes were consolidated due to a vacancy in the Weleetka rural delivery service, moving the Clearview carrier to the Weleetka office. Postal service from the Clearview office was continued until November 17, 1992 when the Postal Department discontinued service from the office after ninety years. However, mail service was provided to the area by the rural carrier from the Weleetka Post Office along with rural route and cluster box service.

Clearview Patriot — Printing Office, Restaurant & BBQ, Gas Station

History

This lot was home to several early Clearview establishments. J. E. Thompson, a prominent promoter and financier in town, financed the town newspaper, the Clearview Patriarch. Between 1904–1917 Clearview had a succession of weekly newspapers known at various times as the Clearview Tribune, the Lincoln, and the Patriarch/Clearview Patriarch. The newspaper was essential for promoting the town throughout the country and encouraging people to move to Clearview.

Over the years there have been restaurants and gas stations including the filling station that Earl and Mallie French opened on this corner in 1927. A 1929 ad lists the Earl French Station as one of 32 filling stations in the area offering Conoco Ethyl Gasoline as distributed by the Okemah Oil and Gasoline Co.

With at least one other gas station operating at this time in Clearview, the presence of these stations implies the ownership of enough cars in Clearview to provide this type of service. Frank Bush would later take over the Earl French Station.
