Clearview Historical Mapping Project / № 003
Sign № 003 · Lot 53a

Miniature Golf Course & Tent Revival Site

In the summer of 1931, Clearview built a miniature golf course — proof that even at the edge of the Great Depression, the town knew how to gather, celebrate, and make something from nothing.

History

This small lot, partially obstructed by the railroad running diagonally through it, served as a gathering place for religious and social activities in Clearview prior to the Great Depression.

The invention of a cheap artificial green in the 1920s spurred a national interest in miniature golf and in the summer of 1931 Nannie Talton and W.L. Bush sponsored the opening of the Smartonian Golf Club in Clearview. The golf course was managed through the Smartonian Club, which sought to 'improve and stimulate community recreation' in the town. They hosted games, performances, Juneteenth events, and built recreation grounds to hold community picnics and programs and likely tent revivals.

This type of cultural programming demonstrates Clearview's thriving community at the peak of the town's population and economic production just before the devastating impact of the Great Depression here and across the nation.
